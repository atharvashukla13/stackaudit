# Go-To-Market Strategy — StackAudit

## Target Audience

**Primary:** Engineering managers and startup CTOs at seed-to-Series B companies (5-50 engineers) who are scaling AI tool adoption without a procurement function.

**Secondary:** Indie hackers and solo founders who personally pay for multiple AI tools and feel the burn rate.

**Why them:** These users make tool purchasing decisions, feel AI cost pressure directly, and are the exact profile Credex serves for discounted credits.

## Distribution Channels (Ranked by Expected ROI)

### 1. Content-Led SEO (Organic)
Publish comparison content targeting high-intent keywords:
- "Cursor vs GitHub Copilot pricing 2026"
- "How much should my team spend on AI tools"
- "AI tool audit template"
- "Reduce AI infrastructure costs"

**Why:** These are research-intent queries from people actively evaluating spend. A free audit tool is the natural next step from reading a comparison post.

### 2. Community Seeding
Share the tool in communities where our target users congregate:
- **Hacker News** — "Show HN: Free audit for your AI tool spending"
- **r/ExperiencedDevs** — "We built a tool to audit AI IDE spend, found most teams overpay by 30%"
- **Indie Hackers** — "How I audit my AI stack (and saved $200/mo)"
- **X/Twitter** — Launch thread with real audit screenshots

**Why:** These communities value free tools and transparent pricing data. The "audit" framing signals trust, not sales.

### 3. Product Hunt Launch
Launch with a clear hook: "StackAudit — See where your team overspends on AI tools"
- Category: Developer Tools, AI, Finance
- Target: Top 5 product of the day → generates sustained traffic

### 4. Cold Outreach (Credex Sales Enablement)
Credex sales team can use StackAudit as a conversation starter:
- "Run your free audit → here's what we found → Credex can save you more"
- Much better than cold "buy credits from us"

## Positioning Statement

> **For** engineering teams spending $500+/mo on AI tools  
> **Who** don't have time to audit their stack  
> **StackAudit** is a free 60-second audit  
> **That** shows exactly where you overspend and how to fix it  
> **Unlike** manually comparing pricing pages  
> **We** give specific, finance-literate recommendations with confidence levels
